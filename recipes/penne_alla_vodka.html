Penne Alla Vodka

Description

This Penne Alla Vodka is a classic and easy-to-make pasta dish featuring penne in a flavorful vodka-infused tomato sauce made with ingredients like prosciutto, tomato paste, cream, and seasonings.

The recipe emphasizes a restaurant-quality taste achieved through simple steps and flavor-enhancing additions, finished with Parmesan cheese.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb of Penne
  • 28 oz of Crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup of Heavy cream
  • 3/4 cup of Vodka
  • 1/4 lb of Roughly chopped prosciutto
  • 2 tsbp of Tomato paste
  • 3 cloves of Minced garlic
  • 1/2 cup of Grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 tsbp of Olive oil

Steps

  1. Saut√© the prosciutto in olive oil in a skillet over medium heat until it begins to brown, which should take about 2-3 minutes. Then, set the prosciutto aside and roughly chop it once it has cooled.
  2. Turn the heat off and add the vodka to the same skillet. Then, set the heat to medium and use a silicone spatula to scrape the bottom and sides of the skillet. Let the vodka bubble gently and reduce by half, which will take about 4-5 minutes or until you can no longer smell the alcohol.
  3. Add the butter and minced garlic to the skillet and cook for 1-2 minutes. Then, add the tomato paste, chicken broth, chicken bouillon, honey, hot sauce, and the seasonings (basil, dried oregano, red pepper flakes). Stir to combine these ingredients.
  4. Add the cooked prosciutto back into the skillet. Then, add the crushed tomatoes. Bring the sauce to a boil, then reduce the heat to low to maintain a gentle simmer. Let it bubble gently and reduce, partially covered, while you begin to boil salted water for the pasta.
  5. Once the salted water is boiling, cook the penne for 1 minute less than the package directions suggest for al dente. Before draining the pasta, reserve about 1 cup of the pasta water. Then, drain the pasta.
  6. Reduce the heat under the sauce to low and stir in the heavy cream until it is fully combined.
  7. Add the cooked penne to the sauce and stir well to combine, ensuring all the pasta is coated.
  8. Add the reserved pasta water as needed to reach your desired consistency. The pasta water helps the sauce cling to the pasta and adds a smooth finish.
  9. Remove the skillet from the heat and, if desired, swirl in 1 tablespoon of cold butter just before serving. This optional step adds a smooth and sparkly finish to the sauce, a technique called "monte au beurre". Serve immediately with freshly grated Parmesan cheese.
